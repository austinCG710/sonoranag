The Problem

Arizona Growers Can't Afford to Miss a Spray Window

Monsoon Mud Delays

Arizona's summer monsoon season saturates fields for days. Ground rigs sit idle while pest and disease pressure builds. By the time the soil dries, the optimal application window has closed.

Soil Compaction from Heavy Equipment

Irrigated desert soil is especially vulnerable to compaction. Heavy boom sprayers crush soil structure, reduce water infiltration, and can cut yields by up to 20% in compacted zones.

Crop Duster Scheduling & Drift

Aerial applicators are booked weeks out during peak season. When they do fly, application happens 50-100+ feet above the canopy — chemical drift is significant, and precision is limited.

Sonoran Precision Ag eliminates these problems. Our drone fleet flies 8-15 feet above your crop canopy, applying product with centimeter-level RTK GPS accuracy — on your schedule, without ever touching the ground.
